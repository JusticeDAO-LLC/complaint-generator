How The Process Works

A simpler guided path through the complaint generator

The homepage should tell a client what to do first, what to do next, and where to go when they return. These steps are the complaint process in plain language.

1

Start intake

Capture the parties, timeline, protected activity, adverse action, and harm in a guided form before trying to draft anything.

2

Organize evidence

Add documents and testimony to the complaint record so the system can connect facts to the claim elements they support.

3

Review support gaps

Use the review surface to see what legal elements are still weak before relying on a draft as final.

4

Draft and revise

Generate the complaint only after the factual record is organized, then refine the result in the editor.

Before You Begin

What a client should know before using this tool

The homepage should set expectations clearly, rather than throwing a client into technical navigation without context.

What to gather first

  • Names of the parties and the organization you are complaining against.
  • A short timeline of what happened and when it happened.
  • Messages, documents, notes, or testimony that support the complaint.
  • The harm you suffered and what relief you may seek.

How this site helps

  • It keeps intake, evidence, review, drafting, and editing connected through one complaint session.
  • It saves progress on this device through the same browser identity used across the complaint generator.
  • It helps organize the complaint, but the result should still be reviewed by counsel or the filing person before submission.
