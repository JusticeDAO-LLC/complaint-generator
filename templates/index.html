How The Process Works

A clear client-facing path through the complaint generator

The homepage now frames the complaint process the way a client expects: collect the facts, organize the evidence, review the legal support, then work on the draft.

1

Tell the story

Begin in the workspace and answer the core intake questions about parties, timeline, harm, and adverse action.

2

Add supporting proof

Attach testimony and documents to the correct claim elements instead of treating evidence as an afterthought.

3

Review weak points

Use the support review surface to identify missing corroboration, proof gaps, and next evidence steps.

4

Draft and revise

Generate the complaint, refine it in the editor, and return to the evidence record whenever the draft needs support.

Before You Begin

What a client should expect on this site

This homepage should set expectations clearly rather than sounding like an internal engineering dashboard.

What To Have Ready

  • Names of the parties involved and the organization you are complaining against.
  • A short timeline of what happened, including dates if you have them.
  • Documents, messages, policies, or notes that help support the complaint.
  • The specific harm you suffered and the relief you may seek.

What The System Will Do

  • Preserve your progress in a resumable browser-based complaint session.
  • Connect intake answers, evidence, support review, and drafting into one workflow.
  • Route you into the correct page instead of forcing you to guess which dashboard to use next.
  • Keep the same complaint state available through the website, CLI, package APIs, and MCP tools.
