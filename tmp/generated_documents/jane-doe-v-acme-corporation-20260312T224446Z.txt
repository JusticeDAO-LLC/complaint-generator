COMPLAINT

NATURE OF THE ACTION
This is a civil action arising from the facts disclosed during the complaint intake process. Plaintiff seeks relief for employment discrimination, retaliation within federal jurisdiction.
The action implicates the following areas of law: employment law, civil rights law.
The draft relies on the following principal legal authorities: 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-2, 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-3.

PARTIES
Plaintiff: Jane Doe.
Defendant: Acme Corporation.

JURISDICTION AND VENUE
This Court has subject-matter jurisdiction under federal law, including 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-2, because Plaintiff alleges violations arising under the laws of the United States.
Venue is proper in the Northern District of California because a substantial part of the events or omissions giving rise to these claims occurred there.

SUMMARY OF FACTS
1. Plaintiff complained to human resources about race discrimination (See Exhibit B).
2. Defendant terminated Plaintiff shortly after the complaint (See Exhibit A).
3. What happened after you reported discrimination?: I was fired two days later and lost my pay and benefits (See Exhibit A).
4. Plaintiff reported discrimination to human resources and was terminated two days later. Plaintiff seeks reinstatement, back pay, and injunctive relief (See Exhibit B).

CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

COUNT I - Employment Discrimination
Legal Standard:
- Membership in a protected class.
- Adverse employment action.
- Discriminatory motive or disparate treatment.
- 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-2 - Title VII of the Civil Rights Act - Prohibits discrimination in employment.
Supporting Facts:
1. Evidence shows facts supporting employment discrimination (See Exhibit A).
2. Termination email and HR complaint timeline (See Exhibit A).
3. Element supported: Adverse employment action (See Exhibit A)
4. Title VII of the Civil Rights Act (See Exhibit C)
Open Support Gaps:
- Discriminatory motive
Exhibits Incorporated by Reference: Exhibit A, Exhibit C.

COUNT II - Retaliation
Legal Standard:
- Protected activity.
- Materially adverse action.
- Causal connection between the activity and the adverse action.
- 42 U.S.C. § 2000e-3 - Title VII anti-retaliation provision - Prohibits retaliation for protected complaints.
Supporting Facts:
1. Evidence shows facts supporting retaliation (See Exhibit B).
2. Termination email and HR complaint timeline (See Exhibit B).
Exhibits Incorporated by Reference: Exhibit B.

REQUESTED RELIEF
1. Reinstatement or front pay in lieu of reinstatement.
2. Back pay, front pay, and lost benefits.
3. Injunctive relief to prevent continuing violations.
4. Compensatory damages in an amount to be proven at trial.
5. Pre- and post-judgment interest as allowed by law.
6. Reasonable attorney's fees and costs where authorized.
7. Injunctive and declaratory relief sufficient to stop the unlawful conduct.
8. Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
